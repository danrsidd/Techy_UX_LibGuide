New to Techy? Start here. - All About Techy

Techy was created in late 2019 to remedy the research related confusion among technological academics, or those pursuing undergraduate academic careers in disciplines like Information Technology & Informatics (ITI). As technological academics, we may not be adequately prepared to pursue research endeavors in accredited academic formats. Our lack of research instruction and awareness of useful research resources and their methods of utilization, hinder our abilities to gather the most effective information for our endeavors. As such, we may settle, or 'satisfice' our information needs, limiting the potential of our efforts. Techy aims to change this. Connecting students with a plethora of open-source, academically driven, and crowd-sourced content, Techy believes utilizing these resources in unison can greatly benefit each and every ITI student.

Search Tips

Making Research Efficient: Mastering the Art of Search!

Boolean: Selecting what YOU want

Boolean search is a search method that can enable you to gain greater control over the relevance of your results. Often times, we resort to simple search queries in a general search engine. Such a query yields millions of results, many of which are not directly relevant to our search. An example of such a query could be ‘Apple news.’ As a ‘techy,’ you may recognize that through such a query, a searcher is likely intending to reference the notable technological corporation, Apple. However, a search engine may not necessarily recognize this. Thus, it may quickly become apparent that some results refer to the fruit, rather than the corporation. Utilizing the above Boolean operators can help limit such irrelevancy. In continuing with the aforementioned example, we can remedy the search by adjusting the query to the following: Apple news NOT fruit.

Curious about more ways to experiment with these operators? Try it out yourself on most popular search engines or databases. Check out the video below for more detailed explanations of Boolean search logic. You’ll quickly realize that such logic, although prominent in search, finds its foundations in many software methodologies, thereby building on some of your technology knowledge!

Command Line Search: Programming Meets Search

Command line search echo’s many of the stylistic elements and logic of basic programming. As you dive deeper into your technological disciplines, you may find command line searching to be an engaging and efficient way of searching a database. For databases which allow command line search, queries are often placed within a variety of field codes, or abbreviations of sort. These field codes are defined within database documentation and represent a variety of elements related to a particular record (filters and facets). An example search on educational technology and classroom collaboration is depicted below. Note the length of the query, in which search terms are placed within the parameters of particular field codes.

  • ProQuest Command Line Search
    Resource outlining the extent of the command line search function on ProQuest databases.
  • Factiva Query Genius
    Test out some command line searches of your own on Factiva and utilize its Query Genius tool. This tool contains advanced syntax features that aims to assist one throughout the command line search process.

DIY Search Engine

Google is often a techy’s first stop in their search journey…and sometimes the only! As such, it is paramount that you be able to maintain control and relevancy no matter the tool you use. In Google’s case, this control is in the form of Google Custom Search Engine (CSE). A CSE is essentially a locked-down version of Google, limited to search only the sites you specify. You can choose to limit your search engine to exclude images or videos, or like traditional Google, include a little bit of everything. The CSE below is set to specifically search all of the suggested technology news publications mentioned in the Staying Up-to-Date tab. Prominent Technology Career Paths

Data Science - Utilizing computing technologies to extract value from datasets.

Data Analytics - In a manner similar to data science, data analytics aims to extract value from raw and unrefined datasets.

Cybersecurity - Utilizing technical skills to protect computers from malicious attacks and unauthorized accesses. 

Front-end Development - Engage in projects centered around user experience and graphics by constructing the looks of applications.

Back-end Development - Apply your technical expertise to large streams of user application data and build a strong application foundation.

Automation - Work to simplify workflows and complex tasks utilizing self-executing computer processes.

Machine Learning - Utilize advanced algorithms and automated technologies to enable independent machine predictions based on particular datasets.

Artificial Intelligence - Implement technical methodologies to enable seamless computational decisions and responses based on collected information.

Databases - Participate in the constriction of extensive databases, which represents the foundation for a solidified back-end system.

Project Management - Lead a team of skilled technologists towards project completion, while remaining up-to-date on all project developments.
